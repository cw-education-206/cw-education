About Us

As part of the course ‘Education, Literacy, and Justice’, where we talked about pedagogy and social change in equal measure, a certain question was inevitably prioritised: how do we make pedagogy effect social change? Unfortunately, much of what we study and research in the university is exclusively curated for specialised audiences. Further, the process of working and researching itself is often too individualised and isolating, and focussed on producing a narrow idea of an academic document. Instead, should we not take on the challenge of producing knowledge in collaborative, dialogic, and more accessible ways, to engage with existing communities and build newer ones?

The public pedagogy project is an attempt to translate the perspectives of the final papers written by the batch into creative presentations for a diverse non-academic public. For many, the process towards developing the project entailed interactions and activities with different groups of people, to both learn from them as well as involve them in the project. The result has been diverse and wide-ranging, from podcasts to infographics to community workshops to fiction and documentary videos. Hope you like them!
